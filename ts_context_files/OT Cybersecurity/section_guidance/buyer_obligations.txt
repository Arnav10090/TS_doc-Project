## buyer_obligations
Role: Lists what Buyer must provide or arrange for the project to succeed. Contractually important.
Required: All standard OTCSS Buyer obligations; any project-specific obligations from metadata.
Infer: All of the following are standard for OTCSS: designate a project SPOC for daily liaison; respond to technical queries in a reasonable time; arrange all Buyer-scope hardware (aggregation switches, server room, power, rack space) well ahead of schedule; provide network cables and accessories not in Seller supply; provide site access for Seller's representatives at agreed times; maintain a dedicated internet connection for remote support; execute NDA before remote support; provide existing network diagrams, asset lists, and policies; enable site entry and safe discovery windows; supply authorized and controlled access to OT environments; make third-party OEM engineers available at site during commissioning if required; coordinate with plant stakeholders (CISO, OT heads, SOC team) during site activities; review and approve deployment plans, architecture, and security configurations; approve scope and objectives; endorse governance and reporting cadence; configure OT firewall rules and SPAN settings to enable IDS sensor traffic capture; in case of health issues to Seller's representative, Buyer provides best available medical facility (expenses borne by Seller).
Never invent: Prerequisites for systems not in metadata.
Structure: Bulleted imperative list ("Provide...", "Arrange...", "Designate...", "Ensure...", "Enable...", "Configure..."). Each item must be specific enough to be contractually referenceable.
