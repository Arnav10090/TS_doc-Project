Implementation phases: M1 — Project Kick-off & Site Audit (~2w): backup of existing HMI applications, verification of current IP addresses and network topology; M2 — Hardware Procurement & Engineering (~6-8w): ordering new IPCs, migrating HMI project files to new software version, resolving conversion errors; M3 — FAT (~1w): demonstration of migrated screens on new hardware (offline, no live PLC data); M4 — Installation & Cutover (~2w): physical replacement of hardware, connection to live PLCs, I/O verification; M5 — Handover (~1w): Operator familiarization, as-built documentation. Total durations indicative.

FAT: Conducted at Hitachi India premises. Involves navigating the migrated screens to ensure visual fidelity and correct tag bindings (tags will show offline/wireframe without live PLCs).

Buyer obligations: Provide complete backups of existing HMI applications (.mcp, .apa, etc.); provide administrative passwords for existing HMIs; provide a scheduled downtime window for the physical hardware swap and IP address cutover.

Value addition: Resolution of up to 10 existing "nuisance alarms" during the migration process.

Standard exclusions: Modifications to PLC logic; redesigning HMI graphics (it is a 1-to-1 migration, not a redesign); replacing field instruments or network cabling.

Risks: Proprietary or custom ActiveX controls on old HMIs may not be supported on modern OS (Windows 10) and may require redevelopment.
